MODELLING AND IDENTIFICATION OF A DC MOTOR

Motors have wide range of applications in almost all industrial sectors. In order to design a speed or position controller for a motor, its mathematical model is needed which requires its parameter estimation The motor used in this experiment is a permanent-magnet dc machine. The motor shaft is attached to a flywheel and a small three-phase generator, which is connected to a resistive load. This load can be switched on or off with the help of an electronic relay. A power amplifier is used to provide electrical power needed for the motor. The output voltage of the power amplifier is controlled by an analog signal u. The speed of the motor is measured by means of a tacho-generator. The tacho-generator generates a DC voltage signal, which is proportional to the shaft speed. A current sensor measures the current flowing through motor coils.The experiment will be performed using the rapid-prototyping system.
